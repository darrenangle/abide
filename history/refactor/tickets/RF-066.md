# RF-066: Eliminate Vacuous Lexical Passes In NoConsecutiveRepeats And CharacterPalindrome

- Priority: `P1`
- Status: `done`
- Area: `constraint reliability`
- Origin: `PUBLIC-CONSTRAINT-SANITY-SCAN-2026-04-01`

## Problem

Two zero-argument public lexical constraints still have vacuous behavior on
degenerate input. `NoConsecutiveRepeats()` returns `score=1.0` on empty and
single-word input and even `passed=True` on punctuation-only multi-line input.
`CharacterPalindrome()` passes punctuation-only lines because normalization
strips them to empty strings and empty strings compare equal to their reverse.

## Primary Files

- `src/abide/constraints/lexical.py`
- `tests/constraints/test_lexical.py`

## Acceptance Criteria

- `NoConsecutiveRepeats` must require a meaningful word sample and must not
  treat empty or punctuation-only text as a clean pass.
- `CharacterPalindrome` must not treat lines with no alphanumeric content as
  palindromes.
- The reproduced public-constraint degenerate cases must fail with low scores.

## Completed

- Hardened `NoConsecutiveRepeats` so it now requires a meaningful word sample
  and scales score by line adequacy instead of returning `1.0` on empty,
  single-word, or punctuation-only input.
- Hardened `CharacterPalindrome` so normalized-empty lines fail instead of
  counting as palindromes.
- Added direct regressions for the reproduced public-constraint degenerate
  cases and re-ran the zero-argument public-constraint sanity scan cleanly.
