# RF-090: Audit the Validated Full-Surface Corpus for Real Semantic/Structural Compliance Beyond Pass Bits

- Priority: `P1`
- Status: `done`
- Area: `training data audit`
- Origin: `user request`
- Depends On: `RF-089`

## Problem

The full-surface Codex-authored corpus validates `141 / 141` against the
current Abide verifiers, but that only proves consistency with the verifier
surface. It does not prove the samples are non-degenerate in the human sense or
that the verifier is catching the right thing on representative examples.

We needed a review pass that samples the corpus, reruns verification directly,
inspects suspicious rows manually, and records any cases where the dataset
passes while obviously missing the intended spirit of the form.

## Primary Files

- `data/sft/codex_spark_full_surface_validated.jsonl`
- `data/sft/codex_spark_full_surface_validated.summary.json`

## Findings

### Conclusion

The verification is real in the narrow sense that the sampled rows do reverify
cleanly against the current Abide implementations. The corpus is not uniformly
real in the stronger sense of "good SFT warmup data for learning poetic forms."

There is a substantial tail of structurally valid but mechanically degenerate
rows, especially in novelty, exact-count, lexical, and highly templated long
forms. These are verifier-consistent examples, not broad cases of fabricated
validation, but they are still weak warmup targets.

### Representative Direct Reverification

The following sampled rows were re-run directly through the current catalog
constructors and all returned `passed=True`, `score=1.0`:

- `PrimeVerse`
- `NumericalEcho`
- `ModularVerse`
- `PrecisionHaiku`
- `Abecedarian`
- `Mesostic`

- `UniqueUtterance`
- `FreeVerse`
- `Haiku`
- `Ghazal`
- `Villanelle`

### Concrete Examples

Examples that reverify cleanly but are too degenerate to trust as warmup data:

- `PrimeVerse`: all lines are only `"a"` tokens with the correct word counts.
- `NumericalEcho`: the same collapse into `"a"` tokens while satisfying the
  pi-digit pattern.
- `ModularVerse`: three lines of only `"a"` tokens.
- `PrecisionHaiku`: `"a my sun by sky"` scaffolding satisfies counts but is
  visibly synthetic.
- `Abecedarian`: `A quiet field rests` through `Z quiet field rests`; structurally
  correct, but heavily templated.
- `Mesostic`: `spa / too / red / emo`; likely valid under the mesostic letter
  rule, but near-useless as style instruction.
- `Sestina`: end-word rotation is real, but the poem is almost entirely `I walk
  through field by ...` scaffolding.
- `CrownOfSonnets` and `ChantRoyal`: structurally consistent and linked/rhymed,
  but dominated by rhyme-bank swapping instead of natural language.
- `PositionalPoem`: four lines of `Tease`.
- `CombinedChallenge`: `a ae eau ioui`.
- `Villanelle`: a minimal one-word-line scaffold (`light / glow / night / ...`)
  that technically satisfies the enforced structure.

Examples that looked materially better in the sample:

- `FreeVerse`
- `Haiku`
- `Ghazal`
- `UniqueUtterance`

### Quantitative Degeneracy Signals

The audit found strong corpus-level evidence of collapse and templating:

- repeated exact lines across the corpus:
  - `36`: `'a'`
  - `20`: `'a a a'`
  - `16`: `'I walk through the cold field and wait for day'`
  - `13`: `'I walk through the cold field and wait for play'`
  - `12`: `'I walk through the cold field and wait for night'`
  - `12`: `'I walk through the cold field and wait for bright'`
- lowest unique-token-ratio rows:
  - `PrimeVerse`: `0.024` (`41` tokens, `1` unique)
  - `LiteraryBallad`: `0.028` (`36` tokens, `1` unique)
  - `NumericalEcho`: `0.032` (`31` tokens, `1` unique)
  - `OddEvenDance`: `0.037` (`27` tokens, `1` unique)
  - `PindaricOde`: `0.045` (`22` tokens, `1` unique)
  - `Naani`: `0.050` (`20` tokens, `1` unique)
  - `Sestina`: `0.060` (`234` tokens, `14` unique)
  - `ModularVerse`: `0.067` (`15` tokens, `1` unique)

## Decision

`RF-090` closes as an audit. The main finding is not that the validation pass is
fake; it is that a fully verifier-valid corpus can still contain a large tail of
degenerate rows that should not be used uncritically for SFT warmup.

The follow-up is corpus-quality filtering and targeted regeneration, not a claim
that the verification numbers themselves were fabricated.

## Follow-Up

- `RF-091` filters or regenerates low-diversity rows in the full-surface corpus
  and adds heuristic anti-degeneracy gates to the Codex-authored SFT pipeline.
