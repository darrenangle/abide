# RF-091: Filter and Regenerate Degenerate Rows in the Full-Surface Codex Corpus

- Priority: `P1`
- Status: `in_progress`
- Area: `training data quality`
- Origin: `RF-090`
- Depends On: `RF-089`, `RF-090`

## Problem

`RF-090` showed that the full-surface Codex-authored corpus validates cleanly
against the current verifiers but still contains many rows that are too
degenerate, repetitive, or templated to be good SFT warmup data.

The issue is concentrated in novelty, lexical, exact-count, and some long-form
template families. Examples include all-`"a"` count-satisfying poems, repeated
single-line scaffolds, and large long-form rhyme-bank shells that satisfy the
mechanical verifier while teaching weak stylistic priors.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Add corpus-quality heuristics that flag low-value validated rows.
- Record explicit thresholds or form-specific exceptions rather than relying on
  vague "quality" language.
- Produce a filtered or regenerated replacement artifact for the worst affected
  rows.
- Keep structural revalidation mandatory after any regeneration.
- Document residual risk for forms where extreme compression is the nature of
  the form rather than a generation bug.

## Progress

- Landed richer warmup prompt construction so generation tasks ask for an actual
  poem instead of only a structural shell.
- Added anti-degeneracy validation that can reject structurally valid drafts for
  placeholder collapse, dominant repeated tokens, and repeated-line shells.
- Added retry-task generation so rejected drafts can be reissued with the prior
  response plus concrete validator feedback.
- Re-ran the full-surface candidate batch through the stricter gate:
  `103` rows remained accepted and `38` rows were sent to retry in
  `data/sft/codex_spark_full_surface_retry_tasks.jsonl`.

## Latest Result

The first structural-plus-judge pass produces `45` adjudicated retry tasks.
That combined queue keeps `33` heuristic rejects that the small-model judge also
rejected, adds `12` additional rows that heuristics originally accepted but the
judge rejected, and drops `5` heuristic rejects that the judge considered poem-like.

After one full rewrite pass and one targeted second-pass cleanup:

- the rewritten `45`-row batch is structurally and heuristically clean
- the first post-rewrite mini-judge pass accepts `34 / 45`
- the second targeted rewrite pass on the remaining `11` raises that to a final
  curated `38 / 45`
- `7` forms still remain judge-rejected:
  `Canzone`, `ChantRoyal`, `CrownOfSonnets`, `DoubleBallade`, `HoratianOde`,
  `Kyrielle`, and `KyrielleSonnet`

Those seven are long or repetition-heavy forms where prompt-and-retry still
collapses into scaffolds. They likely need a more form-specific generation
strategy rather than another generic retry loop.

As the first form-specific correction, `Abecedarian` now has:

- stronger writer guidance that explicitly requires real opening words and a
  coherent scene
- two curated synthetic seed poems wired into `synthetic_sft`
- an exported clean artifact `data/sft/abecedarian_synthetic_curated.jsonl`
  with `12` verifier-passing prompt/response rows

The full-surface cleanup now also has two explicit downstream constraints:

- full-surface rows can be judged exhaustively via the new
  `--include-all-validated` path in `build_poemness_judge_tasks.py`
- the current warmup baseline is the sharded
  `data/sft/abide_warmup_curated-000*.jsonl` corpus, which intentionally
  excludes the retry-prompt artifacts and the long-form scaffold-like
  replacements until they can be rewritten as clean seed poems
