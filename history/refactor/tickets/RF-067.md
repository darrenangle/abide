# RF-067: Add A Permanent Degenerate Harness For Zero-Argument Public Constraints

- Priority: `P1`
- Status: `done`
- Area: `constraint reliability`
- Origin: `PUBLIC-CONSTRAINT-SANITY-SCAN-2026-04-02`

## Problem

The repo has an ad hoc zero-argument public-constraint sanity scan, but the
scan is not yet a committed test. That leaves the constraint surface weaker
than the form surface, where degenerate reward leaks are already locked down by
`tests/forms/test_degenerate_reward_surface.py`. The latest scan also shows one
remaining loud outlier: `CrossLineVowelWordCount()` scores `0.64` on
punctuation-only input.

## Primary Files

- `src/abide/constraints/lexical.py`
- `tests/constraints/test_degenerate_constraint_surface.py`
- `tests/constraints/test_lexical.py`

## Acceptance Criteria

- The zero-argument exported public constraints must have a committed
  degenerate-input harness.
- The harness must fail if a zero-argument public constraint returns a high
  failed score on the agreed degenerate corpus.
- `CrossLineVowelWordCount` must stop awarding high failed scores to
  punctuation-only or otherwise vacuous input.

## Completed

- Added `tests/constraints/test_degenerate_constraint_surface.py` to keep the
  zero-argument exported constraint surface under a committed degenerate-input
  harness.
- Hardened `CrossLineVowelWordCount` so lines with no meaningful vowel target
  or no tokenized words no longer count as successful comparisons.
- Added direct lexical regressions for the reproduced punctuation-only
  `CrossLineVowelWordCount` case.
