# RF-061: Penalize Underlength Line-Match Reward Leaks In Manual Novel Forms

- Priority: `P1`
- Status: `done`
- Area: `reward integrity`
- Origin: `DEGENERATE-INPUT-AUDIT-2026-04-01`

## Problem

Several manual novel forms award almost all of their score to the per-line
property and only lightly tax missing lines, so a single valid line can still
earn `0.90+` reward while `passed=False`. Reproduced cases include
`QuestionQuest("?")` (`0.932`), `ElementalVerse("gold")` (`0.914`),
`TemporalVerse("today")` (`0.904`), `ExclamationEcho("!")` (`0.914`),
`OddEvenDance("one two three")` (`0.904`), `ThunderVerse("x" * 60)` (`0.932`),
and multiple underlength `WhisperPoem` inputs up to `0.988`.

## Primary Files

- `src/abide/forms/novel.py`
- `tests/forms/test_novel_forms.py`

## Acceptance Criteria

- The affected min-line novel forms must count missing required lines against
  their main content score instead of treating the present lines as a complete
  sample.
- The reproduced single-line and short-input cases must no longer score
  near-perfectly.
- Existing valid examples and non-empty partial-credit behavior should remain
  intact where the poem actually meets the minimum sample size.

## Completed

- Extended the steep-penalty helper used by manual novel forms so minimum-line
  requirements count against the main content score instead of only appearing as
  a light line-count tax.
- Applied the sample-aware scoring to `QuestionQuest`, `WhisperPoem`,
  `ThunderVerse`, `OddEvenDance`, `ElementalVerse`, `TemporalVerse`, and
  `ExclamationEcho`.
- Added direct regressions for the reproduced underlength one-line and short
  sample cases, including the near-complete `WhisperPoem` case that should keep
  moderate partial credit without leaking near-perfect reward.
