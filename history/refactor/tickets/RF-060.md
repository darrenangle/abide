# RF-060: Penalize Low-Sample Semantic Reward Leaks In Uniqueness And Monosyllabic Checks

- Priority: `P1`
- Status: `done`
- Area: `reward integrity`
- Origin: `DEGENERATE-INPUT-AUDIT-2026-04-01`

## Problem

Some semantic primitives still give perfect scores whenever the observed sample
is internally clean, even if there are far too few words for the constraint to
mean anything. Reproductions: `AllWordsUnique(min_words=10).verify("alpha")`
and `.verify("alpha bravo")` both return `score=1.0, passed=False`;
`MonosyllabicOnly(min_words=10).verify("cat dog")` returns
`score=1.0, passed=False`; `UniqueUtterance` still leaks `0.904` on `"alpha"`
and `0.914` on `"alpha\nbravo"`; `MonotoneMountain("cat dog")` still returns
`0.482`.

## Primary Files

- `src/abide/constraints/lexical.py`
- `src/abide/forms/novel.py`
- `tests/constraints/test_lexical.py`
- `tests/forms/test_novel_forms.py`

## Acceptance Criteria

- `AllWordsUnique` and `MonosyllabicOnly` must penalize being below their own
  declared minimum word counts instead of returning perfect scores.
- `UniqueUtterance` and `MonotoneMountain` must no longer leak high reward on
  trivially short inputs.
- Existing valid examples for the affected forms and constraints must keep
  passing.

## Completed

- Folded minimum-word adequacy into `AllWordsUnique` and `MonosyllabicOnly`
  scoring so undersized samples no longer receive perfect scores just because
  the observed words are clean.
- Aligned `UniqueUtterance` with its six-line floor by requiring at least six
  unique words for the semantic component to reach full credit.
- Added primitive- and form-level regressions for the reproduced low-sample
  leaks, including `UniqueUtterance` and `MonotoneMountain`.
