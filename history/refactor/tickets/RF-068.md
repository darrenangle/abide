# RF-068: Tighten Structural-Count Decay And Lower The Failed-Score Ceiling Across Exported Forms

- Priority: `P1`
- Status: `done`
- Area: `form reliability`
- Origin: `DEGENERATE-FORM-SCORE-SCAN-2026-04-02`

## Problem

The exported-form degenerate harness is still capped at `0.75`, and a fresh
scan shows a cluster of remaining `0.60+` failed scores from exact-count forms
and a few manual structural proxies. The biggest recurring cause is that
near-miss structural counts still decay too softly.

## Primary Files

- `src/abide/constraints/structural.py`
- `src/abide/forms/diamante.py`
- `src/abide/forms/free_verse.py`
- `src/abide/forms/blank_verse.py`
- `tests/forms/test_degenerate_reward_surface.py`

## Acceptance Criteria

- Exported forms must not leak failed scores above the new stricter ceiling on
  the committed degenerate corpus.
- Exact-count structural misses must score materially lower than they do now.
- The targeted remaining outliers from the 2026-04-02 scan must either be
  fixed or explicitly disappear under the stricter harness threshold.

## Completed

- Tightened `LineCount` near-miss decay and strengthened exact shape scoring so
  missing or extra lines no longer keep unusually high failed scores.
- Hardened `FreeVerse`, `Haiku`, `Tanka`, `Naani`, `Limerick`, and related
  shell/proxy forms so structural gate failures materially dampen overall
  scores instead of leaving near-perfect failures in lenient mode.
- Hardened `LinePairSimilarity` against normalized-empty comparisons so
  punctuation-only repetition no longer scores as a perfect match.
- Lowered `tests/forms/test_degenerate_reward_surface.py` from `0.75` to
  `0.55`; the current worst exported-form failed score on the degenerate corpus
  is now `0.55`.
